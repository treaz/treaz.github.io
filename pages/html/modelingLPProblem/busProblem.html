Bus drivers scheduling problem

Consider a bus company scheduling drivers for its buses each day, as illustrated in Table 1 (the first known of the problem). We want it to fulfill it completely. Drivers work 8 hour shifts that start at times: 0, 4, 8, 12, 16 or 20. The drivers are paid a fix rate (RT) per 8 hours (second known of the problem).

Identify the unknowns (identify the decision variables)

The decision variable is the number of drivers allocated every 4 hours.
X0, X4, X8, X12, X16, X20.
Mathematical notation: Xi, i={0, 4, 8,.. 20}

Formal expression including the unknowns (identify the objective function)

The objective function is the cost of paying all the bus drivers for a day. In this case we want to minimize the cost.
C(ost) = X0*RT + X4*RT + X8*RT+ ...
Mathematical form: C(ost) = sum(Xi*RT); i = {0, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20}

Business limitations imposed on the unknowns (identify the constraints)

The constraints ensure that the drivers allocated for each interval cover the requirement for buses for it.
X20+X0 >= R0; X0+X4 >= R4; X4+X8 >= R8; ...; where Ri is the required number of buses for the interval starting at hour i and ending at hour i+4

Practical limitations imposed on the unknowns (identify the non-negativity constraints)

The non-negativity constraints ensure the number if allocated drivers for an interval is not negative.
X0 >= 0, X4 >= 0, X8 >= 0, X12 >= 0, X16 >= 0, X20 >= 0

The optimal solution (Table 2) is X0=4; X4=10; X8=0; X12=8; X16=4; X20=0.
