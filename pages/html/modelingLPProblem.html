Product mix - a linear programming (LP) problem

A company produces plain and mexican chips. The chips have to go through three processes: slicing, frying, and packing.

The time required by each type of chips for each process is presented in Table 1. There is a limit on the amount of time available for each process (Table 2).The sell price of the chips is presented in Table 3.

You want to determine what combination of chips to produce in order to yeld the highest profit possible.

Chips problem model

Show me the full model

Decision variable: X_i, where i= {plain, mexican}.

P(rofit) = sum (X_i*SellPrice_i); i= {plain, mexican}, needing to be maximized

sum (X_i*TS_i) <= TSa; i = {plain, mexican}
sum(X_i*TF_i) <= TFa; i = {plain, mexican}
sum(X_i*TP_i) <= TPa; i = {plain, mexican}

X_i >= 0; i = {plain, mexican}
