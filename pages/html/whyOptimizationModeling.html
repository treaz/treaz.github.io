Context

Managers are confronted with decisions all the time. Managerial decision making is a process that, most of the time, depends on the intuition and on the experience of the manager. Optimization modeling brings objectivity and more insight into this process and also indicates what is the most profitable decision.
Below we show a simplifed example of a common decision problem: the product mix problem.

You probably see a pattern emerging: as more and more variables are added to the decision process, it is harder and harder to find the most profitable solution.
The current decision problem does not have yet the complexity of a real situation, and even if it would have, there are harder decisions out there than choosing how many chips of each type to produce.

Optimization modeling allows you to create a model of any decision problem and, with the help of a computer, find out what the most profitable decision is.
