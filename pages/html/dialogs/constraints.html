Constraints

Description

An inequality (e.g. a>b, a>=b) or equality (e.g. a=b) defining limitations on decision variables.

Example (from the bus scheduling problem)

  • The constraints ensure that the drivers allocated for each interval cover the requirement for buses for it: X20+X0 >= R0; X0+X4 >= R4; X4+X8 >= R8; ...; where Ri is the required number of buses for the interval starting at hour i and ending at hour i+4
