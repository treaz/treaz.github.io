Linear programming (LP)

Consists of optimization models made up from linear equations and linear inequalities. The feasible decisions are compared using a linear objective function that depends on the decision variables.

Examples

The product mix problem is concerned, for a product portfolio, with how many units of each product should be manufactured.

The resource allocation problem is concerned with how to distribute scarce resources among alternative activities.

The blending/mixing problem is concerned with how to blend or mix ingredients to obtain a product with certain characteristics or properties.
